The Remarkable Legacy of Warren Bennis

2017/3/22

Warren Bennis was one of the great pioneers in the field of leadership.

Just as Peter Drucker was the father of management, Bennis was the father of leadership.

Bennis was the first scholar who said leadership is not a set of genetic characteristics, but the result of a lifelong process of self-discovery.

The most dangerous leadership myth is that leaders are born--that there is a genetic factor to leadership. This myth asserts that people simply either have certain charismatic qualities or not. That's nonsense; in fact, the opposite is true. Leaders are made rather than born.

It is not just a superficial question of style, but has to do with who we are as human beings, and with the forces that have shaped us. The process of becoming a leader is much the same as the process of becoming an integrated human being.

Douglas McGregor, author of The Human Side of Enterprise.

Abraham Maslow (creator of Maslow's Hierarchy of Needs)

Erik Erikon, whose theories on the eight stages of human development influenced Bennis's own generativity in his later years.

I was never going to be able to be happy with positional power. What I really wanted was personal power: having influence based on my voice. My real gift is what I can do in the classroom and as a mentor.

On Becoming a Leader

Finally, I had found a philosophy of leadership I could resonate with.
